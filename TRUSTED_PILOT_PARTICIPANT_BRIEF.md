# Trusted Pilot Participant Brief

## What this product is

This product is an evolving support tool designed to help a person:
- identify their current state
- try a calmer first action
- log what happened
- slowly build personalized patterns over time

It is meant to feel supportive, readable, and cautious.

---

## What this product is not

This product is **not**:
- a medical device
- a diagnosis tool
- a crisis authority
- a finished or fully validated system

It is still in a structured product-hardening stage.

---

## Why you are being invited

You are being invited because this is a **small trusted pilot**, not a broad public rollout.

The goal is to learn:
- whether the main support flow is understandable
- whether the product feels calming or too dense
- whether personalization feels meaningful
- whether the trust language feels honest and clear
- where the product still creates confusion or friction

Your participation helps shape the product while it is still being refined carefully.

---

## What you may be asked to do

Depending on the session, you may be asked to:
- choose a current state
- review starter supports or tailored supports
- log whether something helped
- optionally write a short reflection
- review the Learn Me screen
- explore customization such as state labels or pinned comforts

You may also be asked to react to:
- wording
- trust cues
- uncertainty language
- personalization behavior
- overall ease of use

---

## What the product is trying to learn from use

The product learns from things like:
- outcomes you log
- optional reflections
- journal entries
- support patterns over time

The product is not meant to pretend certainty too early.
It is supposed to grow more trustworthy only as evidence grows.

---

## Important caveats

Please treat the product as:
- a structured support companion
- an evolving tool
- something that may still have rough edges

Please do **not** treat it as:
- final authority
- medical advice
- emergency guidance
- proof that a pattern is permanently true

If something feels unclear, confusing, or too strong in tone, that is important feedback.

---

## What kind of feedback is most helpful

The most helpful feedback is specific.

Examples:
- “I did not know what to click next.”
- “This felt calming.”
- “This wording sounded too certain.”
- “I understood Help Now, but Learn Me felt too abstract.”
- “This personalized part felt useful.”
- “I did not understand why this comfort tool was prioritized.”

You do not need to be polite at the expense of clarity.
Clear feedback is the goal.

---

## What to do if something feels off

If something feels:
- confusing
- too dense
- too certain
- emotionally off-tone
- not useful

Please say so directly.
That is part of why this pilot exists.

---

## Session expectations

This pilot is not a test of you.
It is a test of the product.

You are not expected to get everything “right.”
If something is hard to understand, that may indicate a product problem, not a user problem.

---

## Consent and comfort

Only continue in the pilot if you feel comfortable doing so.
You can pause, stop, or decline parts of the session if needed.

The goal is careful product learning, not pressure.

---

## In one sentence

This pilot is a careful attempt to learn whether the product is understandable, honest, calming, and useful enough to keep refining, not a claim that it is already finished.
