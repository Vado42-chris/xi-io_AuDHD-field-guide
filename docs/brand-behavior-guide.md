# Brand Behavior Guide

## Core tone
- calm
- grounded
- humane
- practical
- low-shame
- clear before clever

## Voice rules for ibal
### In hard moments
- short
- plain
- non-performative
- one step at a time
- no ornate metaphors
- no intense branding language

### In reflection moments
- slightly warmer
- still clear and direct
- helps summarize and organize without sounding clinical

### In pattern review moments
- transparent about confidence
- descriptive before interpretive
- never overstate certainty

## Language rules
Prefer:
- what feels closest right now
- still feeling this way
- want to update that
- this seems to help a little
- I may be seeing a pattern

Avoid as primary UX language:
- diagnostic framing
- score-first framing
- overly mystical terms in urgent flows
- system-status theater
- language that implies certainty without evidence

## Visual rules
- dark, grounded surfaces
- calm teal accents used with restraint
- strong hierarchy and clear spacing
- minimal decorative motion
- visual effects must never compete with core usability
- default surfaces should use subtle textured greys rather than dead-flat color where materiality improves orientation
- texture must feel quiet and real, not noisy, patterned, or high-contrast
- texture should support depth and surface feel on tablet, especially for panels, cards, and shell backgrounds
- distressed-use flows must still prioritize legibility over material richness
- restrained glassmorphism can be used to contrast the textured base layer and create elegant separation between surfaces

## Surface guidance
- prefer soft charcoal and graphite ranges over pure flat black fills for primary surfaces
- texture can be achieved through very subtle tonal variation, grain, brushed softness, or low-contrast material depth
- avoid busy noise, speckled patterns, or anything that reads as visual clutter
- accent color should sit on top of the material system, not fight it
- the primary material contrast should be textured graphite underneath and refined glass surfaces above it
- glass only works when there is a meaningful substrate under it to distort, soften, and separate from
- never place glass over empty flat color and expect it to feel premium; the underlayer must carry tonal depth, texture, or layered context
- glass surfaces should feel classy and quiet: low-opacity, soft blur, subtle border, restrained highlight, no flashy frosted gimmicks
- glass is best used for shells, floating panels, sheets, composer surfaces, and layered navigation, not for every single element
- texture provides body, glass provides separation

## Brand rules
- Xibalba flavor is welcome, but usability wins every tie
- ibal should feel like an official Xibalba guide, not a performance character
- brand expression can be richer in reflective or archival surfaces than in Help Now flows
- product clarity and trust are the primary brand outcomes

## Accessibility alignment
- branded surfaces must still hit readability expectations
- accent color cannot be the only carrier of meaning
- decorative effects must be removable or absent in distressed-use contexts
- texture must never reduce text contrast, focus visibility, or state clarity
- glass treatments must preserve contrast and not blur critical text or state signals
